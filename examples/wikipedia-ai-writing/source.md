## Summary

A Wikipedia project page describing stylistic and structural patterns associated with AI-generated writing. The page was created as part of an ongoing AI-content cleanup effort on Wikipedia. It is explicitly described as a descriptive guide to observed patterns, not a prescriptive ruleset and not a reliable AI detector.

## Extracted signal categories

### Vocabulary signals

Certain words appear with unusually high frequency in suspected AI-generated Wikipedia content:
- delve, underscore, commendable, notable
- "it is important to note that"
- "stands as" used for rhetorical elevation
- "not just X but Y" constructions
- "groundbreaking", "transformative", "landmark"

### Structural signals

- Rigid outline-like endings: "challenges and future prospects", "implications for the field", "further research is needed"
- Formulaic transitions at the start of paragraphs: "Furthermore," "Moreover," "In conclusion,"
- Significance claims appended to otherwise factual sentences without evidence
- Unsupported vague attributions: "experts say", "studies show", "it has been noted"

### Prose quality signals

- Undue coherence: prose reads as a flattened summary, lacking rough edges, voice, or distinctive framing
- Superficial analysis: factual claims followed by an evaluative sentence that adds no specific evidence
- Promotional tone: framing that emphasises importance without supporting facts

## Caveats from the source

- These are signals, not proof.
- The page is descriptive, not prescriptive.
- Not every flagged phrase implies AI authorship.
- Automated AI detectors are explicitly cautioned against.
- Human judgment is always required.
